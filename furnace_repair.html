What are Common Signs of a Furnace/Heater in Need of Repair?

Even when your home furnace system is still heating your home, there are common warning signs HVAC heating and Air Conditioning knows to look for. If you notice any of the following, a heating system problem may be present and it's time to call the professionals at HVAC heating and Air Conditioning for a broken furnace repair:

路 Is Your Furnace On But Not Producing Enough Heat? Is your home furnace unit on but not giving off enough heat during winters day? Your furnace may have a clogged, dirty air filter, one of the main causes of a furnace breakdown.

路 Is Your Home's Furnace Making Drastic Temperature Changes or Not Heating at All? Your gas furnace could be experiencing a thermostat failure or malfunction. A faulty thermostat can lead to fan, pilot light, power, or gas issues & ruin the comfort of your home. Restore comfort levels by giving HVAC heating and Air Conditioning a call to get a furnace repair diagnostic before any work is done.

路 Home Furnace Systems in Orange County, CA are Prone to Crack and rust. Over time this may cause your gas furnaces' heat exchanger to crack or rust through, a potentially very dangerous situation as cracks can allow carbon monoxide to seep into your home. Call HVAC heating and Air Conditioning furnace expert right away to have your home's furnace professionally diagnosed & repaired.

路 Experiencing Pilot Light or Electric Ignition Issues? If your furnace isn't turning on at all, a common explanation is an issue with your pilot light or electronic ignition. Older furnace heating systems with a pilot light that continues to go out may seem like a small problem, but it can actually be wasting a lot of money on your monthly gas bill. As the weather gets colder, a flickering pilot light, will make your furnace work harder and cause unnecessary wear & tear. and a much more expensive bill.

No matter the problem, our HVAC Orange county furnace repair technicians will determine what needs to be repaired before any work is done, so you know exactly what solution is best for your home furnace system.
