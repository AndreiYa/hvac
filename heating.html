Shopping for a new furnace can be intimidating. Our goal is to take the stress out of shopping. We'll help you find a furnace that fits your home, heating preferences and budget. Factors that affect cost

· Furnace efficiency: The AFUE (Annual Fuel Utilization Efficiency) rating measures how efficiently the furnace turns gas into heat. The higher the AFUE rating, the more energy efficient—and also more expensive—the furnace will be.

· Furnace size: The bigger the furnace's heating capacity, the more expensive the furnace will be. Your technician will help you determine the size you need for your home.

· Comfort features: If you add advanced comfort features like a variable-speed blower, modulating gas burner or a insulated blower compartment, the installation cost will increase.

What are Common Signs of a Furnace/Heater in Need of Repair?

Even when your home furnace system is still heating your home, there are common warning signs HVAC heating and Air Conditioning knows to look for. If you notice any of the following, a heating system problem may be present and it's time to call the professionals at HVAC heating and Air Conditioning for a broken furnace repair:

· Is Your Furnace On But Not Producing Enough Heat? Is your home furnace unit on but not giving off enough heat during winters day? Your furnace may have a clogged, dirty air filter, one of the main causes of a furnace breakdown.

· Is Your Home's Furnace Making Drastic Temperature Changes or Not Heating at All? Your gas furnace could be experiencing a thermostat failure or malfunction. A faulty thermostat can lead to fan, pilot light, power, or gas issues & ruin the comfort of your home. Restore comfort levels by giving HVAC heating and Air Conditioning a call to get a furnace repair diagnostic before any work is done.

· Home Furnace Systems in Orange County, CA are Prone to Crack and rust. Over time this may cause your gas furnaces' heat exchanger to crack or rust through, a potentially very dangerous situation as cracks can allow carbon monoxide to seep into your home. Call HVAC heating and Air Conditioning furnace expert right away to have your home's furnace professionally diagnosed & repaired.

· Experiencing Pilot Light or Electric Ignition Issues? If your furnace isn't turning on at all, a common explanation is an issue with your pilot light or electronic ignition. Older furnace heating systems with a pilot light that continues to go out may seem like a small problem, but it can actually be wasting a lot of money on your monthly gas bill. As the weather gets colder, a flickering pilot light, will make your furnace work harder and cause unnecessary wear & tear. and a much more expensive bill.

Heater/Furnace maintenance

Why do I need regular maintenance on my heating system?

Regular furnace maintenance by a reliable contractor like HVAC Heating and Air Conditioning is an important factor in preparing your system to perform at its best. Regular service is vital if you want to avoid unnecessary equipment failure and repair costs. It’s a good idea to have a HVAC technician check and service yours every spring and fall.

How often should I change my furnace air filter?

We recommend changing your disposable filter every 3 months. Likewise, if you have washable filters, you should clean them every 3 months. A filter that’s become clogged with dust, pet dander, mold, pollen, or any number of pollutants and particles puts a strain on your cooling system and spoils the air your family breathes.

Ductless and Mini-Split Ductless

For decades, split-zoning air-conditioning and heat pump systems have been the quiet solution for cooling and heating problems around the world. These powerful systems have three main components: an indoor unit, outdoor unit, and wireless controller.

Why Ductless?

A ductless, mini-split system is an option for cooling:

· Homes without ductwork or with poor ductwork. Frequently these are older buildings constructed before air conditioning existed.

· Homes with boilers or other methods of hot water heat

· Room additions, such as an added upper level or three-season porch.

· High level of customization.

Features

· Ideal for “zoning” rooms in your home or for cooling individual rooms, each of which can have its own thermostat.

· Reduce energy loss sometimes associated with duct work.

· A heat pump can sometimes be incorporated to provide both heating and cooling.

· Anti-allergen filter prevents the cross-flow of air with contaminants.

· Whisper-quiet operation.

Installation

· Indoor air handlers can be suspended from a ceiling, mounted flush into a drop ceiling, or hung on a wall. This is also a good option for room additions where duct work is not feasible.

· These systems don’t require ductwork, so they can be installed in a wide variety of settings, including homes with plaster walls and brick facades.

· These powerful systems have three main components: an indoor unit, outdoor unit, and wireless controller. The compressor and evaporator components are housed in separate units.
